## Overview


Engineered a high-integrity **relational database system** simulating a multi-location retail business across **10 locations** and **400+ transactions** to generate realistic, actionable business intelligence.

Focused on analyzing **revenue trends**, **inventory performance**, and **customer behavior**, transforming raw transactional data into insights that support real-world decision-making.

---

## Database Design

Built a **normalized relational schema** with interconnected tables including **Customers, Orders, OrderDetails, Products, Suppliers, Categories, Employees, Locations, and Returns**.

Implemented **primary and foreign key relationships** to maintain data integrity and support accurate relationships across the system.

Structured the database to support **scalable querying and reporting**, making it possible to analyze multiple areas of business performance efficiently.

---

## Data Analytics Layer

Developed **17+ SQL queries** to analyze key business metrics including **monthly, weekly, and yearly revenue**, **inventory performance**, **customer purchasing patterns**, and **sales distribution across locations**.

Applied core SQL techniques including **multi-table joins**, **aggregations** such as **SUM, COUNT, and AVG**, and **subqueries** to extract meaningful insights from relational data.

Improved performance through the use of **indexes**, supporting faster query execution on larger datasets.

---

## Key Insight

Identified how a smaller group of products could drive a disproportionate share of revenue, highlighting opportunities for **inventory optimization**, **smarter stocking decisions**, and more targeted sales analysis.

This project demonstrates how structured SQL analysis can uncover patterns that directly influence business strategy.
